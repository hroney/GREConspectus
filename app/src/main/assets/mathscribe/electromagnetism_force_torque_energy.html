Force, torque and energy

$●$ Electromagnetic force and torque

Force between two static charges: Coulomb's law $\bo F_2={q_1q_2}/{4πε_0r^2_{12}}\bo r_{12}$
Force between two current-carrying elements $d\bo F_2={μ_0I_1I_2}/{4πr^2_{12}}[d\bo l_2,[d\bo l_1,\bo r_{12}]]$
Force on a current-carrying element in a magnetic field $d\bo F=Id\bo l ×\bo B$
Force on a charge (Lorentz force)$\bo F=q(\bo E+[\bo v ,\bo B])$
Force on an electric dipole$^a$$\bo F=(\bo p ,\bo ∇)\bo E$
Force on an magnetic dipole$^b$$\bo F=(\bo m ,\bo ∇)\bo B$
Torque on an electric dipole$\bo G=[\bo p ,\bo E]$
Torque on an magnetic dipole$\bo G=[\bo m ,\bo B]$
Torque on an current loop$\bo G=I_L ∮↙{\text"loop"}[\bo r ,[d\bo l_L ,\bo B]]$

$^a$ $\bo F$ simplifies to $\bo ∇(\bo p ,\bo E)$ if $p$ is intrinsic, $\bo ∇(pE\text"/"2)$ if $\bo p$ is induced by $\bo E$ and the medium is isotropic.

$^b$ $\bo F$ simplifies to $\bo ∇(\bo m ,\bo B)$ if $m$ is intrinsic, $\bo ∇(mB\text"/"2)$ if $\bo m$ is induced by $\bo B$ and the medium is isotropic.

where

$\bo F_2$force on $q_2$ or $d\bo l_2$$q_{1,2}$charges
$\bo r_{1,2}$vector from 1 to 2 $ε_0$permittivity of free space
$dl_{1,2}$line element$I_{1,2}$currents flowing along $d\bo l_1$ and $d\bo l_2$
$μ_0$permeability of free space$F$force
$I$current flowing along $d\bo l$ $\bo B$magnetic flux density
$\bo E$electric field$\bo v$charge velocity
$\bo p$electric dipole moment$\bo m$magnetic dipole moment
$\bo G$torque$dl_L$line element (of loop)
$\bo r$position vector $dl_L$$I_L$current around loop

$●$ Electromagnetic energy

Electromagnetic field energy density (in free space) $u={1}/{2}ε_0E^2+{1}/{2}{B^2}/{μ_0}$
Energy density in media$u={1}/{2}\((\bo D,\bo E)+(\bo B,\bo H)\)$
Energy flow (Poynting) vector$\bo N=[\bo E ,\bo H]$
Mean flux density at a distance $r$ from a short oscillating dipole $\ov \bo N={ω^4p_0^2\sin θ}/{32π^2ε_0c^3r^3}\bo r$
Total mean power from oscillating dipole$^a$$W={ω^4p_0^2\text"/"2}/{6πε_0c^3}$
Self-energy of a charge distribution $U_{tot}={1}/{2}∮↙{\text"volume"}φ(\bo r)ρ(\bo r)dτ$
Energy of an assembly of capacitors$^b$$U_{\text"tot"}={1}/{2}∑↙{i}∑↙{j}C_{ij}V_iV_j$
Energy of an assembly of inductors$^c$$U_{\text"tot"}={1}/{2}∑↙{i}∑↙{j}L_{ij}I_iI_j$
Intrinsic dipole in an electric field$U_{\text"dip"}=-(\bo p,\bo E)$
Intrinsic dipole in an magnetic field$U_{\text"dip"}=-(\bo m,\bo B)$
Hamiltonian of a charged particle in an EM field$^d$ $H={|\bo p_m-q\bo A|^2}/{2m}+qφ$

$^a$ Sometimes called "Larmor's formula".

$^b$ $C_{ii}$ is the self-capacitance of the $i$th body. Note that $C_{ij}=C_{ji}$.

$^c$ $L_{ii}$ is the self-inductance of the $i$th body. Note that $L_{ij}=L_{ji}$.

$^d$ Newtonian limit, i.e. velocity $≪c$.

where

$u$energy density$\bo B$magnetic flux density
$\bo E$electric field$ε_0$permittivity of free space
$μ_0$permeability of free space$\bo D$electric displacement
$\bo H$magnetic field strength$c$speed of light
$N$energy flow rate per unit area $⊥$ to the flow direction$p_0$ amplitude of dipole moment
$\bo r$vector from dipole ($≫$wavelength) $θ$angle between $\bo p$ and $\bo r$
$ω$oscillation frequency$W$total mean radiated power
$U_{\text"tot"}$total energy$dτ$volume element
$\bo r$position vector of $dτ$$φ$electrical potential
$ρ$charge density$V_i$potential of $i$th capacitor
$C_{ij}$mutual capacitance between capacitors $i$ and $j$ $L_{ij}$mutual inductance between inductors $i$ and $j$
$U_{\text"dip"}$energy of dipole$\bo p$electric dipole moment
$\bo m$magnetic dipole moment$H$Hamiltonian
$\bo p_m$particle momentum$q$particle charge
$m$particle mass$A$magnetic vector potential
