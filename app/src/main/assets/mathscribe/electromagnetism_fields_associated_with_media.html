Fields associated with media

$●$ Polarisation

Definition of electric dipole moment$\bo p=q\bo a$
Generalised electric dipole moment$\bo p=∫↙\text"volume"\bo r' ρdτ'$
Electric dipole potential$φ(\bo r)={\bo p⋅\bo r}/{4πε_0r^3}$
Dipole moment per unit volume (polarisation)$^a$$\bo P=n\bo p$
Induced volume charge density$\bo ∇⋅\bo P=-ρ_{ind}$
Induced surface charge density$σ_{ind}=\bo P⋅\bo s$
Definition of electric displacement$\bo D=ε_0\bo E+\bo P$
Definition of electric susceptibility$\bo P=ε_0χ_E\bo E$
Definition of relative permittivity$^b$$\table ε_r=1+χ_E ; \bo D=ε_0ε__r\bo E; =ε\bo E$
Atomic polarisability$^c$$\bo p=α\bo E_{loc}$
Depolarising fields$\bo E_d=-{N_d \bo P}/{ε_0}$
Clausius -Mossotti equation$^d$${nα}/{3ε_0}={ε_r-1}/{ε_r+2}$

$^a$ Assuming dipoles are parallel.

$^b$ Relative permittivity as defined here is for a linear isotropic medium.

$^c$ The polarisability of a conducting sphere radius $a$ is $α=4πε_0a^3$. The definition $\bo p=αε_0\bo E_{loc}$ is also used.

$^d$ With the substitution $η^2=ε_r$ this is also known as the "Lorentz-Lorenz" formula.

$$φ$$dipole potential$$ε_0$$permittivity of free space
$$\bo r$$vector from dipole$$\bo P$$polarisation
$$n$$number of dipoles per unit volume $$ρ_{ind}$$volume charge density
$$σ_{ind}$$surface charge density $$\bo s$$unit normal to surface
$$\bo D$$electric displacement$$\bo E$$electric field
$$χ_E$$electrical susceptibility (may be tensor) $$ε_r$$relative permittivity
$$ε$$permittivity$$α$$polarisability
$$\bo E_{loc}$$local electric field $$\bo E_{d}$$depolarising field

$●$ Magnetisation

Definition of magnetic dipole moment$d\bo m=Id\bo s$
Generalised magnetic dipole moment $\bo m=1/2 ∫↙\text"volume"\bo r' ×\bo J dτ'$
Magnetic dipole (scalar) potential $φ_{m}(\bo r)={μ_0\bo m⋅\bo r}/{4πr^3}$
Dipole moment per unit volume (magnetisation)$^a$$\bo M=n\bo m$
Induced volume current density$\bo J_{ind}=\bo ∇ ×\bo M$
Induced surface current density$\bo j_{ind}=\bo M ×\bo s$
Definition of magnetic field strength, $\bo H$ $\bo B=μ_0 (\bo H +\bo M )$
Definition of electric susceptibility$\table \bo M=χ_H\bo H ; ={χ_B}/{μ_0} \bo B ; χ_B={χ_H}/{1+χ_H}$
Definition of relative permeability$^b$$\table \bo B =μ_0μ_r\bo H ; =μ\bo H; μ_r=1+χ_B ; ={1}/{1-χ_B}$

$^a$ Assuming dipoles are parallel.

$^b$ Relative permeability as defined here is for a linear isotropic medium.
