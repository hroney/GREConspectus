Perturbation theory

$●$ Time-independent perturbation theory

Unperturbed states$H↖{∧}_0\ψ_n=E_n\ψ_n$
$(\ψ_n \text" nondegenerate")$
Perturbed Hamiltonian$H↖{∧}=H↖{∧}_0+H↖{∧}'$
Perturbed eigenvalues$^a$$E'_k=E_k + ‹\ψ_k∣{H'}↖{∧}∣\ψ_k›$
$+∑↙{n≠k}{∣ ‹\ψ_k∣{H'}↖{∧}∣\ψ_n› ∣^2}/{E_k-E_n}+...$
Perturbed eigenfunctions$^b$$\ψ'_k=\ψ_k+∑↙{n≠k}{‹\ψ_k|{H'}↖{∧}|\ψ_n›}/{E_k-E_n}\ψ_n+...$

$^a$ To second order.

$^b$ To first order.

where

$H↖{∧}_0$unperturbed Hamiltonian$\ψ_n$eigenfunction of $H↖{∧}_0$
$E_n$$\text"eigenvalues of " H↖{∧}_0$$H↖{∧}$perturbed Hamiltonian
$n$integer $≥0$$H↖{∧}'$perturbation $(≪H↖{∧}_0)$
$E'_k$perturbed eigenvalue $(≈E_k)$$‹∣∣›$Dirac bracket
$\ψ'_k$perturbed eigenfunction $(≈ψ_k)$

$●$ Time-dependent perturbation theory

Unperturbed stationary states$H↖{∧}_0\ψ_n=E_n\ψ_n$
Perturbed Hamiltonian$H↖{∧}(t)=H↖{∧}_0+H↖{∧}'(t)$
Schrodinger equation$[H↖{∧}_0+H↖{∧}'(t)]\Ψ(t)=\iħ{∂\Ψ(t)}/{∂t}$
$\Ψ(t=0)=\ψ_0$
Perturbed wavefunction$^a$$\Ψ(t)=∑↙{n}c_n(t)\ψ_n\exp{(-\iE_nt\text"/"ħ)}$
where
$c_n={-\i}/{ħ}∫↙{0}↖t ‹\ψ_n∣{H'}↖{∧}(t')∣\ψ_0›\exp{[\i(E_n-E_0)t'\text"/"ħ]}dt'$
Fermi's golden rule$\Γ_{i→f}={2π}/{ħ}∣‹\ψ_f∣{H'}↖{∧}∣\ψ_i›∣^2 ρ(E_f)$

$^a$ To first order.

where

$H↖{∧}_0$unperturbed Hamiltonian$\ψ_n$eigenfunction of $H↖{∧}_0$
$E_n$$\text"eigenvalues of " H↖{∧}_0$$H↖{∧}$perturbed Hamiltonian
$n$integer $≥0$$H↖{∧}'(t)$perturbation $(≪H↖{∧}_0)$
$t$time$\Ψ$wavefunction
$\ψ_0$initial state$ħ$$\text"(Planck constant)/"(2π)$
$c_n$probability amplitudes$\Γ_{i→f}$transition probability per unit time from state $i$ to state $f$
$ρ(E_f)$density of final states
