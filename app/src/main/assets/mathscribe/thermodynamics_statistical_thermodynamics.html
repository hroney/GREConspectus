Statistical Thermodynamics

$●$ Statistical entropy

Boltzmann formula$^a$$S=k\ln W$
$≈k\ln g(E)$
Gibbs entropy$^b$$S=-k∑↙{i}p_i\ln p_i$
$N$ two-level system$W={N!}/{(N-n)!n!}$
$N$ harmonic oscillators$W={(Q+N-1)!}/{Q!(N-1)!}$

$^{a,b,c}$

$^a$ Sometimes called "configuration entropy".

$^b$ Sometimes called "canonical entropy"."

where

$S$entropy$k$Boltzmann constant
$W$number of accessible microstates$g(E)$density of microstates with energy $E$
$∑_{i}$sum over microstates$p_i$probability that the system is in microstate $i$
$N$number of systems$n$number in upper state
$Q$total number of energy quanta available

$●$ Ensemble probabilities

Microcanonical ensemble$^a$$p_i=1/W$
Partition function$^b$$Z=∑↙{i}e^{-βE_i}$
Canonical ensemble (Boltzmann distribution)$^c$$p_i=1/Ze^{-βE_i}$
Grand partition function$Ξ=∑↙{i}e^{-β(E_i-μN_i)}$
Grand canonical ensemble (Gibbs distribution)$^d$$p_i=1/Ξe^{-β(E_i-μN_i)}$

$^{a,b,c}$

$^a$ Energy fixed.

$^b$ Also called "sum over states".

$^c$ Temperature fixed.

$^d$ Temperature fixed. Exchange of both heat and particles with a reservoir.

where

$p_i$probability that the system is in microstate $i$$∑_{i}$ sum over microstates
$W$number of accessible microstates$Z$partition function
$β$$=1\text"/"{(kT)}$$E_i$energy of microstate $i$
$k$Boltzmann constant$T$temperature
$Ξ$grand partition function$μ$chemical potential
$N_i$number of particles in microstate $i$

$●$ Macroscopic thermodynamic variables

Helmholtz free energy$F=-kT\ln Z$
Grand potential$Φ=-kT\ln Ξ$
Internal energy$U=F+TS=-{∂\ln Z}/{∂β}|_{V,N}$
Entropy$S=-{∂F}/{∂T}|_{V,N}={∂(kT\ln Z)}/{∂T}|_{V,N}$
Pressure$p=-{∂F}/{∂V}|_{T,N}={∂(kT\ln Z)}/{∂V}|_{T,N}$
Chemical potential$μ={∂F}/{∂N}|_{V,T}=-{∂(kT\ln Z)}/{∂N}|_{V,T}$

where

$F$Helmholtz free energy$Φ$grand potential
$Z$partition function$Ξ$grand partition function

$●$ Identical particles

Bose-Einstein distribution$^a$$f_i=1/{e^{β(ε_i-μ)}-1}$
Fermi-Dirac distribution$^b$$f_i=1/{e^{β(ε_i-μ)}+1}$
Fermi energy$^c$$ε_F={ħ^2}/{2m}\({6π^2n}/{g}\)^{2\text"/"3}$
Bose condensation temperature$T_c={6πħ^2}/{mk}\[{n}/{gζ(3\text"/"2)}\]^ {2\text"/"3}$

$^{a,b,c}$

$^a$ For bosons $f_i≥0$.

$^b$ For fermions $0≤f_i≤1$.

$^c$ For noninteracting particles. At low temperatures, $μ≈ε_F$.

where

$f_i$mean occupation number of $i$th state$β$$=1\text"/"{(kT)}$
$ε_i$energy quantum for $i$th state$μ$chemical potential
$ε_F$Fermi energy$ħ$$(\text"Planck constant)/"(2π)$
$n$particle number density$m$particle mass
$g$spin degeneracy $(=2s+1)$$ζ$Riemann zeta function $ζ(3\text"/"2)≈2.612$
$T_c$Bose condensation temperature
