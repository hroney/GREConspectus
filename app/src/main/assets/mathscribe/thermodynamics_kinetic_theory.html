Kinetic theory

$●$ Monatomic gas

Pressure$p=1/3nm{\ov c^2}$
Equation of state of an ideal gas$pV=NkT$
Internal energy$U=3/2NkT=N/2m{\ov c^2}$
Heat capacities$C_V=3/2Nk$
$C_p=C_V+Nk=5/2Nk$
$γ={C_p}/{C_V}=5/3$
Entropy (Sackur- Tetrode equation)$^a$$S=Nk\ln{[({mkT}/{2πħ^2})^{3\text"/" 2}e^{5\text"/"2}V/N]}$

$^{a,b,c}$

$^a$ For the uncondensed gas. The factor $({mkT}/{2πħ^2})^{3\text"/"2}$ is the quantum concentration of the particles $n_Q$. Their thermal de Broglie wavelength $λ_T$, approximately equals $n_Q^{-1\text"/"3}$

where

$p$pressure$n$number density $(=N\text"/"V)$
$m$particle mass${\ov c^2}$mean squared particle velocity
$V$volume$k$Boltzmann constant
$T$temperature$U$internal energy
$γ$ratio of heat capacities $(C_p\text"/"C_V)$ $N$number of particles
$C_p$heat capacity, constant $p$ $C_V$heat capacity, constant $V$
$S$entropy$ħ$$=\text"(Planck constant)/"(2π)$
$e$$=2.71828...$

$●$ Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution$^1$

Particle speed distribution$\text"pr"(c)dc=({m}/{2πkT})^{3\text"/"2} \exp{({-mc^2}/{2kT})}4πc^2dc$
Particle energy distribution$\text"pr"(E)dE={2E^{1\text"/"2}}/ {π^{1\text"/"2}(kT)^{3\text"/"2}}\exp{({-E}/{kT})}dE$
Mean speed$\ov c=({8kT}/{πm})^{1\text"/"2}$
rms speed$c_{rms}=({3kT}/{m})^{1\text"/"2}=({3π}/{8})^{1\text"/"2}\text" "\ov c$
Most probable speed$c↖{∧}=({2kT}/{m})^{1\text"/"2}=({π}/{4})^{1\text"/"2}\text" "\ov c$

$^{a,b,c}$

$^1$ Probability density functions normalised so that $∫_{0}^{∞}\text"pr"(x)dx=1$.

where

$\text"pr"$probability density$m$particle mass
$c$particle speed$E$particle kinetic energy $(=mc^2\text"/"2)$
$\ov c$mean speed$c_{rms}$root mean squared speed
$c↖{∧}$most probable speed

$●$ Gas equipartition

Classical equipartition$^a$$E_q=1/2kT$
Ideal gas heat capacities$C_V=1/2fNk=1/2fnR$
$C_p=Nk(1+f/2)$
$γ={C_p}/{C_V}=1+2/f$

$^{a,b,c}$

$^a$ System in thermal equilibrium at temperature $T$.

where

$E_q$energy per quadratic degree of freedom$f$number of degrees of freedom
$R$molar gas constant$n$number of moles
