$●$ Complex numbers

Cartesian form: $z=x+iy$
Polar form: $z=re^{iθ}=r\(\cos θ+i\sin θ\)$
Modulus$^1$:$|z|=r=√{x^2+y^2}$
$|z_1⋅z_2|=|z_1|⋅|z_2|$
Argument: $θ=\arg z=\arctan ({y}/{x})$
$\arg (z_1z_2)=\arg(z_1)+\arg (z_2)$
Complex conjugate: $z^*=x-iy=re^{-iθ}$
$\arg (z^*)=-\arg (z)\text" ; "$ $z⋅z^*=|z|^2$
Logarithm$^2$: $\ln z=\ln r +i\(θ+2πn\)$

$^{a,b,c}$

$^1$ Or "magnitude"

$^2$ The principal value of $\ln z$ is given by $n=0$ and $-π< θ ≤π$

then ${∂ u}/{∂ x}={∂ v}/{∂ y}$ and ${∂ u}/{∂ y}=-{∂ v}/{∂ x}$

Chauchy integral formula$^d$ $f(z_0)={1}/{2πi}∮↙{c} {f(z)}/{z-z_0}dz$ $f^{(n)}(z_0)={n!}/{2πi}∮↙{c} {f(z)}/{\(z-z_0\)^{n+1}}dz$
Laurent expansion$^e$$f(z)=∑↙{n=-∞}↖∞ a_n \(z-z_0\)^n$
where $a_n={1}/{2πi} ∮↙{c} {f(z')}/{\(z'-z_0\)^{n+1}}dz'$

$^a$ Closed contour integrals are taken in the counterclockwise sense, once.

$^d$ If f(z) is analytic within and on a simple closed curve $c$, encircling $z_0$.
$^e$ Of f(z), (analytic) in the annular region between concentric circles $c_1$ and $c_2$, centred on $z_0$. $c$ is any closed curve in this region encircling $z_0$.
