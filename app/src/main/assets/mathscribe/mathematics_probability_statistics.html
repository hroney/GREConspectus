Probability and statistics

$●$ Discrete statistics

Mean$\ov x={1}/{N}∑↙{i=1}↖N x_i$
Variance$^a$$\text"var"\[x\]={1}/{N-1}∑↙{i=1}↖N \(x_i-\ov x\)^2$
Standard deviation$σ\[x\]=√{\text"var"\[x\]}$
Skewness$\text"skew"\[x\]={N}/{(N-1)(N-2)}$ $⋅∑↙{i=1}↖N \({x_i-\ov x}/{σ}\)^3$
Kurtosis$\text"kurt"\[x\]≈\[{1}/{N}∑↙{i=1}↖N \({x_i-\ov x}/{σ}\)^4\]-3$
Correlation coefficient$^b$ $r={∑↙{i=1}↖N \(x_i-\ov x)(y_i-\ov y)}/{√{∑↙{i=1}↖N \(x_i-\ov x)^2}√{∑↙{i=1}↖N \(y_i-\ov y)^2}}$

$^{a,b,c}$

$^a$ If $\ov x$ is derived from the data, {$x_i$}, the relation is as shown. If $\ov x$ is known independently, then an unbiased estimate is obtained by dividing the right-hand side by $N$ rather then $N-1$.
$^b$ Also known as "Pearson's $r$".

where

$x_i$data series$σ$Standard deviation
$N$series length$x,y$data series to correlate
$\ov x$mean value$r$correlation coefficient
$var\[x\]$unbiased variance
